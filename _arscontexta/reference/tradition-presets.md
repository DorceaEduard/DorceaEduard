# Methodology Tradition Presets

Named points in configuration space. Starting points for derivation, not templates to copy.

## Zettelkasten
Deep synthesis, connection density, long-term accumulation.
- Granularity: atomic | Organization: flat | Linking: explicit, bidirectional
- Processing: heavy | Navigation: 3-4 tier | Maintenance: continuous
- Schema: moderate | Automation: convention
- Best for: Research, academic work, long-term intellectual projects.

## PARA (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archive)
Project execution, actionable organization, quick filing.
- Granularity: coarse | Organization: hierarchical (4 folders) | Linking: minimal
- Processing: light | Navigation: 2 tier | Maintenance: condition-based
- Schema: minimal | Automation: manual
- Best for: Project management, task-heavy workflows, storage-oriented systems.

## Evergreen Notes (Andy Matuschak)
Evolving understanding, continuous refinement, living knowledge.
- Granularity: atomic | Organization: flat | Linking: explicit, contextual
- Processing: heavy (continuous rewriting) | Navigation: 3 tier
- Maintenance: continuous | Schema: moderate | Automation: convention
- Best for: Personal knowledge development, conceptual exploration, writing.

## Cornell Note-Taking System
Structured review, testing effect, retention.
- Granularity: medium (per-session) | Organization: temporal
- Linking: implicit (cue columns) | Processing: heavy (5 Rs)
- Navigation: 2-3 tier (index-based) | Maintenance: spaced review
- Schema: moderate | Automation: convention
- Best for: Learning, study, courses, structured knowledge acquisition.
